Personal Story

My Travel
Experience

Last year, I traveled to Cartagena de Indias with my family, and it turned out to be one of the most unforgettable trips of my life. We set off early in the morning and arrived at the walled city by noon. The first thing I noticed was the colorful architecture of the old town ‚Äî every street looked like a painting.

"We walked through the historic center and visited the Castillo de San Felipe de Barajas, a massive fortress that once protected the city from pirates."

Our guide explained the history in detail, and I was completely fascinated. In the evenings, we tried fresh seafood at a local restaurant near the port and watched the sunset over the Caribbean Sea.

On the last day, we took a boat trip to the Islas del Rosario, where the water was so crystal-clear that I could see the colorful fish swimming beneath the surface. I swam, snorkeled, and completely forgot about my worries. When we finally headed back home, I felt refreshed and deeply grateful for the experience. Visiting Cartagena showed me how rich and beautiful Colombia's history and natural landscapes truly are.

Destinations

Where to Go

Cartagena has several must-visit places that will leave you speechless. Here are the top spots you should not miss:

01
Ciudad Amurallada

The walled Old City is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Walk along the historic walls, admire colonial architecture, and soak in centuries of Caribbean history.

02
Castillo de San Felipe

One of the most impressive Spanish fortresses in the Americas, built to defend the city from pirate attacks. The views from the top are absolutely breathtaking.

03
Islas del Rosario

A stunning archipelago about 35 km from the city with beautiful coral reefs, perfect for snorkeling and diving in crystal-clear Caribbean waters.

04
Bocagrande

The modern beach neighborhood where tourists can relax by the sea, enjoy fresh fruit cocktails, and watch the vibrant city life pass by.
